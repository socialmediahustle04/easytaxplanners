## High-Level Image Strategy

ClearTax uses visuals to sell speed, automation, scale, and confidence. The homepage does not rely on a single illustration. It layers several kinds of visual proof:

- Human cutout photography to make tax filing feel light and approachable.
- Floating product/data cards to imply automation and low manual effort.
- Large trust numbers and rating pills to imply scale.
- Service cards with soft icons to segment different taxpayer types.
- Carousel-style proof panels to show accuracy, expert support, and app/product convenience.
- Small process UI mockups to explain how filing becomes easy.
- Expert-help banners and pricing tables to route users into assisted plans.
- Review cards and brand-logo areas to reinforce credibility.

The emotional message is: "You can file quickly, accurately, and safely because a large platform has already simplified the hard parts."

EasyTaxPlanners should not copy that message directly. Our safer strategic takeaway is: visual proof matters. EasyTaxPlanners should show checklist clarity, CA-led review, deadline confidence, and human accountability, not platform scale or instant automation.

## Hero Visual Analysis

### What Is Shown

Desktop hero structure:
- Left side: rating pill, large headline, refund/guarantee strip, notice-protection strip, and two prominent action cards.
- Right side: a large smiling person holding a phone, backed by a circular "accuracy" trust ring.
- Floating card near the person: auto-fetching data from PAN, Form 16, and AIS, with rows for salary, F&O profit, and capital gains.
- Lower band: large refund/user numbers.

Mobile hero structure:
- The large person/product visual is mostly removed from the first viewport.
- Rating, headline, guarantee strip, two action cards, and proof numbers become the core mobile experience.

### Emotional Message

The hero visual communicates:
- Speed: "File in minutes."
- Automation: data is auto-fetched.
- Accuracy: repeated in headline and circular visual ring.
- Relief: the human photo is relaxed and smiling.
- Safety: notice protection and refund/computation-error reassurance.
- Scale: millions of users and refund numbers.

### Positioning And Approximate Ratios

Desktop:
- Hero occupies a tall first viewport with a two-column composition.
- Text and CTAs use roughly 45-50% of the width.
- Human/product visual uses roughly 40-45% of the width.
- Floating dashboard card is approximately 4:3.
- Human cutout area is roughly 3:4 portrait, visually cropped into a circular badge.
- CTA cards below the headline are approximately 5:4, with strong blue buttons.

Mobile:
- Hero becomes content-first.
- Action cards sit in a two-column row.
- Large portrait visual is removed from primary mobile focus.
- Proof numbers become a simple two-column grid.

### Motion Observations

From screenshot and scroll inspection, motion appears restrained:
- Sticky navigation remains visible.
- Some sections behave like carousels with dots and horizontal overflow.
- Visual panels appear in soft card containers with subtle reveal/slide behavior.
- No heavy video background or complex 3D hero was observed.

Safe lesson: use light motion for attention and hierarchy, but keep critical content visible without scroll-triggered animation.

## Below-The-Fold Visual Sections

### Audience Segmentation Cards

ClearTax follows the hero with "India's Most Trusted Tax Filing Platform For" and a grid of audience cards:
- Salaried professionals
- Investors and traders
- Freelancers and professionals
- Advanced traders
- NRIs and RSU/ESOP holders
- Affluent investors

Visual pattern:
- Soft white or tinted cards.
- Small service/category icons.
- Short copy and CTA buttons.
- On mobile, cards stack vertically as full-width rounded blocks.

Message: "Whatever your tax profile is, we have a route for you."

Safe lesson for EasyTaxPlanners: segment by practical customer type, not by complex investor categories. Use new business owners, freelancers, GST businesses, private companies, pending-filing users, and balance-sheet-for-loan users.

### "Why Choose" Carousel Panels

This section uses wide cards with product proof:
- A centered title.
- Horizontal carousel cards.
- Mixed visuals: product card, human cutout, phone/app screen.

Message:
- Accuracy and support are delivered by technology and expertise.
- The user can trust the platform because the system checks data.

Approximate ratios:
- Carousel panel cards: wide 16:9 to 2:1 bands.
- Image elements inside cards: portrait cutouts or phone mockups.

Safe lesson: one wide visual proof band can explain a key trust promise. EasyTaxPlanners should make this about "review before final filing" or "document checklist before work starts," not automation claims.

### Filing Process Micro-Visuals

The page shows small UI-like cards under a line similar to "Tax filing, as easy as it gets":
- Pre-filled form card.
- Broker/integration steps card.
- Capital gains / F&O style card.

Visual pattern:
- Small product mockups spaced horizontally.
- Minimal icons and document-like cards.
- Dots/steps beneath.

Message:
- Complex tax work is converted into simple steps.
- Data entry and calculation burdens are reduced.

Safe lesson: EasyTaxPlanners should use document checklist, review, and completion status visuals. Avoid implying auto-fetching from government systems unless actually supported.

### Promotional CTA Band

A pale-blue wide banner repeats the core promise and CTA:
- Headline and CTA button on the left.
- Soft brand background.
- Compact trust strip.

Approximate ratio:
- Wide banner around 5:1 on desktop.
- On mobile, becomes a compact stacked card.

Safe lesson: create a mid-page compliance-help CTA, but with original wording and our CA-led review angle.

### Expert Help Section

ClearTax uses a section about expert help:
- Expert image/video-style card.
- Three process cards.
- Possible human/expert portrait.
- Timeline-like panels.

Message:
- If self-service is not enough, experts are available.
- Assisted filing is still structured and fast.

Safe lesson: EasyTaxPlanners should lean into this more strongly than ClearTax because our trust advantage is human CA-led support. Use original imagery of a calm consultation, checklist, and follow-up, not copied expert portraits.

### Pricing And Reviews

Pricing cards:
- Multiple vertical plan cards.
- Colored labels for plan levels.
- Lists of inclusions.
- CTA buttons.

Reviews:
- Horizontal review cards with star ratings.
- User names/locations.
- Soft shadows and repeated card rhythm.

Message:
- Buyers can compare plans and see social proof.

Safe lesson: if pricing is added later, keep it transparent. For V1, focus on enquiry and scope-before-payment instead of fixed pricing tables.

### Logo / Employer Trust Area

A row/grid of faint logo placeholders appears under "trusted by employees from India's biggest brands."

Message:
- People from known companies use the platform.

Safe lesson: EasyTaxPlanners should not fake brand logos or overclaim. Use process trust, CA-led wording, and customer type cards instead.

### FAQ And Footer

FAQ is text-first with accordion rows. Footer is dense and SEO-oriented, with many links.

Safe lesson: EasyTaxPlanners should use resources and FAQ for SEO, but keep V1 navigation simpler and service-led.

## Aspect Ratio Notes

Approximate visual ratios observed:
- Desktop hero image area: wide 16:9 section with right-side 3:4 human cutout.
- Floating dashboard/data cards: 4:3 or 5:4.
- Primary action cards: 5:4.
- Audience cards: 2:1 on desktop, stacked 1.8:1 to 2.5:1 on mobile.
- Carousel feature panels: 16:9 to 2:1.
- Process mini cards: square to 4:3.
- Mid-page CTA banner: 5:1 desktop, stacked card on mobile.
- Expert/consultation image: wide 16:9 banner or 4:3 card.
- Review cards: 3:2 to 2:1.
- Logo rows: small horizontal pills/tiles.

## Recommendations For EasyTaxPlanners

Use original generated imagery in a different direction:

1. Keep the hero as a professional compliance dashboard plus physical document/checklist cues, not a smiling phone-user cutout.
2. Use service-specific visual cards with Indian small-business context and abstracted documents, not platform screenshots.
3. Use CA-led human trust imagery carefully: partial hands, desk, consultation, checklist, laptop, calendar, and WhatsApp-like support bubble without logos.
4. Favor warm professional realism mixed with clean UI overlays. Avoid generic stock-photo office scenes.
5. Use aspect ratios that fit the current site:
   - Hero: 16:10 or 4:3.
   - Service visuals: 4:3.
   - About/contact visuals: 4:3 or 3:2.
   - Resource thumbnails: 16:9.
6. Animations should be subtle:
   - Fade/translate on section entry.
   - Slight hover lift on image cards.
   - Very gentle status/checklist motion if implemented.
   - No dependency on scroll for content visibility.

## Final Takeaway

ClearTax uses images to say "big automated platform." EasyTaxPlanners should use images to say "calm professional guidance from a CA-led team." The better product story for this project is not faster software; it is less confusion, reviewed filing, practical checklist support, and someone accountable when the user is unsure.
