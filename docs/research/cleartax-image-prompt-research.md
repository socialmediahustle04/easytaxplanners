# ClearTax Image Prompt Research

## Purpose And Boundary

This document translates public competitor observation into safe, original image direction for EasyTaxPlanners. It is research only. Do not copy or recreate ClearTax photos, people, dashboard UI, card layouts, icons, colors, typography, copy, animation timing, screenshots, or trade dress.

Evidence used:
- Existing ClearTax visual notes in `docs/research/cleartax-image-positioning-analysis.md`
- Existing ClearTax design notes in `docs/research/cleartax-design-reference.md`
- Existing internal screenshot reference at `d_home_full.png`

## What ClearTax Is Doing Visually

ClearTax uses visuals to sell a large-platform promise: filing is fast, automated, accurate, and trusted by many users. The visual system combines a big headline, proof numbers, human photography, product-style dashboard cards, audience/service cards, expert-assistance modules, plan cards, reviews, logo rows, and FAQ/footer density.

The page does not depend on one image. Instead, each visual reinforces a specific conversion job:

- Hero imagery reduces fear and makes tax filing feel easy.
- Product cards imply automation and structured data handling.
- Trust numbers and rating strips imply market scale.
- Audience cards help users self-identify.
- Expert-help imagery reassures users who do not want to file alone.
- Pricing cards package the decision.
- Review cards and brand-logo areas reduce buyer hesitation.
- Mobile layout keeps the promise, CTA, cards, and proof numbers while reducing the large decorative visual load.

## Major Visual Roles And What They Communicate

### 1. Hero Composition

Desktop role:
- Large left-side promise with CTA cards.
- Right-side human/photo/product proof.
- Floating data-style card to show tax details becoming organized.
- Proof numbers below the fold.

Message:
- "This is fast, accurate, and easy."
- "The platform knows how to handle your data."
- "Many people trust this."

Approximate ratio and placement:
- Overall hero section: wide 16:9 style first-screen area.
- Human/photo area: portrait-like 3:4 within the right side.
- Floating product card: roughly 4:3.
- Action cards: roughly 5:4.

Mobile behavior:
- Large photo/product visual is reduced or removed from the first viewport.
- The hierarchy becomes headline, trust strip, action cards, proof numbers, then audience cards.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- The hero needs a strong proof visual, but ours should communicate organized compliance, CA-led review, checklist clarity, and calm confidence.

Must avoid:
- Human cutout/photo treatment, circular accuracy ring, auto-fetching card, exact CTA-card rhythm, exact blue/white styling, exact scale-proof approach.

### 2. Human / Photo Usage

ClearTax uses friendly human visuals to remove fear and make filing feel approachable. These images behave like trust softeners: the user sees a calm person, not only forms and numbers.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- Human presence can help trust, but it should feel like a real advisory moment: hands reviewing a checklist, a professional desk conversation, a client receiving guidance.

Must avoid:
- Stock-photo smiling-phone-user energy, copied poses, staged corporate handshake visuals, fake certificates, visible professional identity claims not approved by the CA.

### 3. Dashboard / Product Cards

ClearTax uses floating product cards and small UI cards to imply automated data capture, prefilled returns, broker imports, and guided filing.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- Abstract dashboard-like cards are useful visual proof. EasyTaxPlanners should show service selected, checklist ready, pending review, filed/completed, and support available.

Must avoid:
- Any layout that looks like a copied product screenshot or actual tax portal UI.
- Claims such as instant auto-fetching unless the product truly supports them.

### 4. Service / Audience Cards

ClearTax uses audience cards to quickly let users locate themselves: salaried professionals, investors, freelancers, NRIs, traders, affluent investors.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- Use segmentation around practical service needs: income tax filing, GST returns, company filing, GST registration, incorporation, financial statements, pending filings, and loan-related balance sheets.

Must avoid:
- Copying the exact six-card audience grid, icon style, copy structure, or color wash.

### 5. Expert Help Imagery

ClearTax adds expert-help visuals to reassure users who need human assistance. The visual pattern is a mix of expert photos, process cards, and support promise.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- This is central to our positioning. Our imagery should make professional follow-up and CA-led review feel like the main product, not a fallback.

Must avoid:
- Copying expert portraits, video-card styling, or "file in 24 hours" type promises.

### 6. Trust / Review Visuals

ClearTax uses review cards, stars, user counts, refund totals, and brand association areas to create scale proof.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- Trust needs to be visible, but we should use process trust instead of fake scale: checklist before work, scope before payment, review before filing, WhatsApp support, safe first enquiry.

Must avoid:
- Fake logos, fake review cards, fake counts, unverified testimonials, maximum-refund claims, 100% accuracy claims.

### 7. Pricing / Product Cards

ClearTax pricing cards make service packages comparable. They use plan names, inclusions, price anchors, and CTA buttons.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- If pricing is introduced later, it should be transparent and scoped. For the current site, images should support enquiry and checklist clarity rather than fixed plan-selling.

Must avoid:
- Copying plan-card structure or making pricing claims before the CA team confirms service scope.

### 8. Mobile Image Behavior

The mobile page prioritizes content and CTA over large visuals. Hero proof becomes text/card-based; large decorative imagery gets removed, reduced, or pushed below. Service cards stack, proof cards become compact, and footer/link density becomes long-scroll.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- Generated images must crop well. Desktop can use richer visual scenes, but mobile should keep images as supportive cards, not the only way to understand a service.

Must avoid:
- Images with critical information only at the far edges.
- Tiny embedded text that becomes unreadable on mobile.

### 9. Aspect Ratios And Cropping

Observed ratios:
- Hero visual area: 16:9 to 16:10.
- Floating dashboard/product cards: 4:3 or 5:4.
- Action cards: close to 5:4.
- Audience/service cards: 2:1 on desktop, stacked cards on mobile.
- Feature carousel panels: 16:9 to 2:1.
- Process micro visuals: square to 4:3.
- Expert/help image: 16:9 or 4:3.
- Resource/blog thumbnails: safest at 16:9.

EasyTaxPlanners target ratios:
- Hero: 16:10.
- Service images: 4:3.
- About trust image: 3:2.
- Contact image: 4:3.
- Resource thumbnail: 16:9.

### 10. Animation And Scroll Behavior

ClearTax uses sticky navigation, carousel-like panels, dots, horizontally arranged proof cards, hover-like card affordances, and subtle reveal/slide behavior. It does not appear to rely on heavy video backgrounds or complex 3D scenes.

Safe EasyTaxPlanners lesson:
- Use subtle image reveal, hover lift, and soft zoom inside clipped containers after images are integrated.
- Critical content must remain visible without scroll-triggered opacity.

Must avoid:
- Copying animation timing, carousel layout, or exact section rhythm.

## Final Direction For EasyTaxPlanners

The image system should feel like:

> A modern CA office with a calm compliance dashboard.

The images should communicate:
- "You will know what documents are needed."
- "A professional reviews the work."
- "You can ask for help."
- "Your filing/registration has a clear next step."
- "This service is serious, but not scary."

The images should not communicate:
- "This is a giant automation platform."
- "Everything happens instantly."
- "Maximum refund is guaranteed."
- "We have fake scale proof."
- "This looks like another brand."

Every generated image should be original, practical, and section-specific.
